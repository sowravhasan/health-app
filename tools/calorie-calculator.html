How We Calculate Your Calories

1. Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Calories your body needs at rest using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the most accurate for most people.

2. Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

BMR multiplied by your activity level factor to account for physical activity and exercise.

3. Goal Adjustment

Adjusted based on your weight goal: -500 calories for weight loss, +500 for weight gain.

4. Safe Recommendations

Minimum 1200 calories for women and 1500 for men to ensure adequate nutrition.

Weight Management Tips

For Weight Loss

Create a 500-750 calorie deficit daily for safe 1-1.5 lbs/week weight loss. Combine reduced calories with increased activity.

For Maintenance

Eat at your TDEE level. Monitor weight weekly and adjust calories by 100-200 if weight trends up or down.

For Weight Gain

Add 300-500 calories above TDEE for healthy weight gain. Focus on nutrient-dense foods and strength training.
